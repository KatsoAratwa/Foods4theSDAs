All About The Founder

A LITTLE GET TO KNOW ME...

My love for cooking started in the kitchen with my mother, where I learned an important lesson that I still cherish today — always serve a balanced meal. Growing up, food was not just something we ate; it was a way of bringing our family together with care and love.

Originally, I was a member of the Jehovah's Witnesses. However, everything changed when I visited a Seventh-day Adventist Church in Lobatse Central with a close friend. From the moment I entered, I felt a sense of belonging, like I am in my father's house indeed and purpose that I had never experienced before. I knew in my heart that this was the right spiritual home for me.

I began dedicating my time and soul to learning more about the Seventh-day Adventist faith — studying their customs, standards of dress, and especially their teachings about health and food. It was a journey of change: I adapted my diet to align with the Adventist principles of healthy, plant-based eating. Even though I was the only Adventist in my family, I slowly introduced these beautiful principles into our family meals, blending tradition with a new way of life focused on health, balance, and faith. As it says in 1 Corinthians 10:31 "So whether you eat or drink or whatever you do, do it all for the glory of God." This verse always reminds me that our bodies are the temple of the Lord and we have to cherish them.

Through this website, I want to share with current Adventists so that they can get new meal ideas and more importantly New Adventists so that can know what to eat and what not to eat. I hope to share the joy of healthy living with the rest of the world. Amen.

Core Adventist Principles

Sabbath Observance

Principle: The seventh day (Saturday) is the biblical Sabbath and should be kept holy as a day of rest and worship.

"Remember the Sabbath day, to keep it holy." — Exodus 20:8

Practice: Adventists attend church services on Saturday, avoid work or secular activities, and use the day to connect spiritually with God and family.

Healthful Living

Principle: The body is God's temple and should be cared for through proper diet, rest, exercise, and abstaining from harmful substances.

"So whether you eat or drink, or whatever you do, do it all for the glory of God." — 1 Corinthians 10:31

Practice: Many Adventists follow a vegetarian or plant-based diet, avoid alcohol, tobacco, caffeine, and strive for balanced living.

Second Coming of Christ

Principle: Jesus Christ will return soon, personally and visibly, to bring salvation to His faithful followers.

"For the Lord Himself will descend from heaven with a shout..." — 1 Thessalonians 4:16

Practice: Adventists live with a sense of spiritual readiness, spreading the gospel and helping others prepare for Jesus' return.
