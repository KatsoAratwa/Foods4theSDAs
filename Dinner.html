Lentil Shepherd's Pie

⏱ 1 hour 15 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried green or brown lentils
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 1 tsp rosemary
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/4 cup plant-based milk
  • 2 tbsp vegan butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook lentils in vegetable broth until tender (about 25 minutes). Drain excess liquid.
  2. Meanwhile, boil potatoes until soft. Mash with plant milk, vegan butter, salt, and pepper.
  3. Sauté onion, carrots, and celery until soft. Add garlic, herbs, and tomato paste.
  4. Combine cooked lentils and vegetable mixture. Stir in peas.
  5. Spread lentil mixture in baking dish, top with mashed potatoes.
  6. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25-30 minutes until golden.
Alternatives: Use sweet potatoes instead of regular potatoes for topping. Add mushrooms for extra umami flavor.

Quinoa Stuffed Bell Peppers

⏱ 50 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers
  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1/2 cup vegan cheese (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut tops off peppers and remove seeds.
  2. Cook quinoa in vegetable broth according to package directions.
  3. Sauté onion and garlic until soft. Add zucchini and cook 3 minutes.
  4. Mix cooked quinoa, sautéed vegetables, black beans, corn, and spices.
  5. Stuff peppers with mixture. Place in baking dish with 1/2 inch water.
  6. Bake 25-30 minutes. Add vegan cheese last 5 minutes if using.
  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.
Alternatives: Use brown rice instead of quinoa. Add diced tomatoes or salsa to the filling.

Chickpea Curry

⏱ 35 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) chickpeas, drained
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 tsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne (optional)
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Cooked brown rice for serving

Instructions:

  1. Sauté onion until translucent. Add garlic and ginger, cook 1 minute.
  2. Add spices and stir for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Pour in coconut milk and tomatoes. Bring to simmer.
  4. Add chickpeas and simmer 15-20 minutes.
  5. Stir in spinach until wilted.
  6. Serve over brown rice, garnished with cilantro.
Alternatives: Use sweet potatoes or cauliflower as additional vegetables. For creamier texture, blend 1/2 cup of chickpeas before adding.

Vegetable Lasagna

⏱ 1 hour 30 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 9 lasagna noodles (whole wheat or gluten-free)
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1 zucchini, thinly sliced
  • 1 eggplant, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cups spinach
  • 15 oz ricotta cheese (or vegan ricotta)
  • 1 egg (or flax egg for vegan)
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella (or vegan cheese)
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Cook noodles according to package.
  2. Mix ricotta with egg (or flax egg), basil, and half the nutritional yeast.
  3. Spread 1/2 cup marinara in 9x13 baking dish. Layer 3 noodles.
  4. Add half the ricotta mixture, then half the vegetables.
  5. Repeat layers: sauce, noodles, ricotta, vegetables.
  6. Top with final noodles, remaining sauce, and mozzarella.
  7. Cover with foil, bake 30 minutes. Uncover, bake 15 more minutes.
  8. Let rest 10 minutes before serving.
Alternatives: Use sliced mushrooms or butternut squash instead of eggplant. For vegan version, use cashew ricotta.

Black Bean Burgers

⏱ 30 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs (or oat flour for gluten-free)
  • 1/4 cup finely diced onion
  • 1/4 cup finely diced bell pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ground flax + 3 tbsp water
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Whole grain buns and toppings of choice

Instructions:

  1. Mix flax and water, set aside to thicken (flax egg).
  2. Mash black beans in large bowl, leaving some texture.
  3. Add all other ingredients including flax egg, mix well.
  4. Form into 6 patties. If too wet, add more breadcrumbs.
  5. Cook in lightly oiled skillet over medium heat 4-5 minutes per side.
  6. Alternatively, bake at 375°F (190°C) for 20 minutes, flipping halfway.
  7. Serve on buns with lettuce, tomato, avocado, etc.
Alternatives: Use pinto beans instead of black beans. Add 1/4 cup corn kernels for sweetness. For nutty flavor, add 2 tbsp sunflower seeds.

Butternut Squash Risotto

⏱ 45 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 small butternut squash, peeled and cubed (about 3 cups)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice
  • 1/2 cup white wine (or vegetable broth)
  • 4-5 cups hot vegetable broth
  • 1/2 tsp sage
  • 1/2 tsp thyme
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast (or Parmesan for non-vegan)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Roast squash cubes at 400°F (200°C) for 20 minutes until tender.
  2. In large pot, sauté onion until soft. Add garlic, cook 1 minute.
  3. Add rice, stir 2 minutes until slightly translucent.
  4. Pour in wine, stir until absorbed.
  5. Add hot broth 1/2 cup at a time, stirring frequently until absorbed before adding more.
  6. When rice is al dente (about 20 minutes), stir in roasted squash, herbs, and nutritional yeast.
  7. Season with salt and pepper. Garnish with parsley.
Alternatives: Use pumpkin instead of butternut squash. For creamier texture, stir in 1/4 cup coconut milk at the end.

Tofu Stir Fry

⏱ 30 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 14 oz extra firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 4 cups mixed vegetables (broccoli, bell peppers, carrots, snap peas)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp sriracha (optional)
  • Sesame seeds and green onions for garnish
  • Cooked brown rice for serving

Instructions:

  1. Toss tofu with cornstarch. Cook in large skillet until crispy, set aside.
  2. In same skillet, sauté garlic and ginger in sesame oil 30 seconds.
  3. Add vegetables, stir fry 5-7 minutes until crisp-tender.
  4. Whisk together soy sauce, maple syrup, broth, vinegar, and sriracha.
  5. Add sauce and tofu to vegetables. Cook 2 minutes until sauce thickens.
  6. Serve over rice, garnished with sesame seeds and green onions.
Alternatives: Use tempeh instead of tofu. For nut-free version, use olive oil instead of sesame oil.

Stuffed Acorn Squash

⏱ 1 hour

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium acorn squash, halved and seeded
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 apple, diced
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup pecans, chopped
  • 1 tsp sage
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Maple syrup for drizzling (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Brush squash with oil, season with salt and pepper.
  2. Place squash cut-side down on baking sheet. Roast 30 minutes until tender.
  3. Meanwhile, cook quinoa in vegetable broth according to package directions.
  4. Sauté onion and celery until soft. Add apple, cook 2 minutes.
  5. Combine cooked quinoa, sautéed mixture, cranberries, pecans, and spices.
  6. Flip squash, fill with quinoa mixture. Bake 10 more minutes.
  7. Drizzle with maple syrup before serving if desired.
Alternatives: Use wild rice instead of quinoa. Substitute walnuts for pecans. Add vegan sausage crumbles for protein.

Vegetable Paella

⏱ 45 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups short-grain rice (Bomba or Arborio)
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 pinch saffron threads
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed
  • 1 cup artichoke hearts, quartered
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • Lemon wedges and parsley for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat broth with saffron in saucepan, keep warm.
  2. In large paella pan or skillet, sauté onion until soft. Add garlic, cook 1 minute.
  3. Add bell peppers and green beans, cook 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in rice, paprika, and turmeric. Cook 1 minute.
  5. Add tomatoes and artichokes. Pour in warm broth.
  6. Simmer without stirring 15-20 minutes until rice is tender.
  7. Add peas last 5 minutes. Remove from heat, cover, rest 5 minutes.
  8. Serve with lemon wedges and fresh parsley.
Alternatives: Add chickpeas for protein. Use brown rice (adjust liquid and cooking time). Include sliced mushrooms for earthiness.

Eggplant Parmesan

⏱ 1 hour 15 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants, sliced 1/4" thick
  • Salt for sweating eggplant
  • 1 cup flour (or gluten-free flour)
  • 2 eggs (or flax eggs for vegan)
  • 2 cups breadcrumbs (or gluten-free)
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast (or Parmesan for non-vegan)
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 3 cups marinara sauce
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella (or vegan cheese)
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped
  • Olive oil for baking

Instructions:

  1. Sprinkle eggplant slices with salt, let sit 30 minutes. Rinse and pat dry.
  2. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Set up breading station: flour, beaten eggs, and breadcrumbs mixed with nutritional yeast and Italian seasoning.
  3. Coat eggplant slices in flour, then egg, then breadcrumb mixture.
  4. Place on oiled baking sheets. Bake 20 minutes, flip, bake 10 more minutes until golden.
  5. In 9x13 dish, layer sauce, eggplant, cheese, repeating. Top with cheese.
  6. Bake 25-30 minutes until bubbly. Let rest 10 minutes before serving.
  7. Garnish with fresh basil.
Alternatives: Use zucchini instead of eggplant. For lighter version, skip breading and just layer roasted eggplant slices.

Mushroom Walnut Tacos

⏱ 25 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 1 cup walnuts, finely chopped
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp taco seasoning
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup vegetable broth
  • 8 corn or whole wheat tortillas
  • Toppings: avocado, lettuce, tomato, salsa, lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. In skillet, sauté onion until soft. Add garlic, cook 1 minute.
  2. Add mushrooms and walnuts. Cook until mushrooms release liquid.
  3. Stir in taco seasoning, soy sauce, tomato paste, and broth.
  4. Simmer 5-7 minutes until liquid is absorbed.
  5. Warm tortillas according to package directions.
  6. Serve taco filling with desired toppings.
Alternatives: Use pecans instead of walnuts. Add black beans for extra protein. For smoky flavor, add 1/2 tsp chipotle powder.

Sweet Potato & Black Bean Enchiladas

⏱ 1 hour

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 8 whole wheat or corn tortillas
  • 2 cups enchilada sauce
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (or vegan cheese)
  • Fresh cilantro and avocado for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss sweet potatoes with oil, roast 20 minutes until tender.
  2. Sauté onion until soft. Add garlic and spices, cook 1 minute.
  3. Mash half the sweet potatoes. Mix with whole cubes, black beans, and onion mixture.
  4. Spread 1/2 cup enchilada sauce in baking dish.
  5. Fill tortillas with sweet potato mixture, roll up, place seam-side down in dish.
  6. Top with remaining sauce and cheese. Bake 20 minutes until bubbly.
  7. Garnish with cilantro and avocado slices.
Alternatives: Add corn kernels or spinach to filling. Use butternut squash instead of sweet potatoes.

Vegetable Pot Pie

⏱ 1 hour 15 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 pie crust (store-bought or homemade, vegan if needed)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup potatoes, diced
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • 1/2 cup frozen corn
  • 3 tbsp flour (or gluten-free flour)
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup plant-based milk
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 1 tsp rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In large pot, sauté onion, carrots, and celery until softened.
  3. Add garlic, potatoes, and mushrooms. Cook 5 minutes.
  4. Sprinkle flour over vegetables, stir to coat.
  5. Gradually add broth and milk, stirring constantly until thickened.
  6. Stir in peas, corn, and herbs. Season with salt and pepper.
  7. Transfer to baking dish. Cover with pie crust, venting top.
  8. Bake 30-35 minutes until crust is golden.
  9. Let cool 10 minutes before serving.
Alternatives: Use biscuit dough instead of pie crust. Add cooked lentils for extra protein.
